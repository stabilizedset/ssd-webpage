What is SSD?

SSD is an algorithmic stablecoin that stabilizes itself to 1 USDC. In order to stabilize, it incentivizes market participants to voluntarily increase or decrease the coin supply. It is decentralized, censorship-resistant and doesn’t use any collateral backing.

How It Works?

Voluntary Supply Allocation

To meet the optimal market demand, SSD’s total supply can either increase or decrease to return SSD back to 1 USDC peg. However, unlike rebase coins SSD doesn’t increase or decrease the coin supply by adjusting the amount of coins in coin holder’s wallet. SSD offers incentives for it’s holders to voluntarily increase or decrease the coin supply. There are two incentives. Buying coupons and providing liquidity.

Increasing Supply to Decrease Price Back To 1 USDC

When SSD trades above 1 USDC peg, SSD Governance mints an appropriate amount of new SSD, increasing the coin supply, in order to push the price back down to 1 USDC. 60% of the newly minted SSD is distributed to SSD holders that have bonded their SSD inside the DAO and the coupon buyers, while the remaining 40% is distributed to Uniswap Liquidity Providers.

Burning Coupons Increase Price Back To 1 USDC

SSD governance issues coupons that expire in 45 days. When SSD trades below 1 USDC peg, holders purchase coupons at a discount price by burning their SSD, thereby reducing circulating supply which in turn increases the price. These coupons are redeemable for newly minted SSD when the price increases above 1 USDC, so it creates the incentive for holder to buy SSD at a lower price, burn part of it and profit from newly minted coins as well as price increase.

How Secure Is It?

Security Audited

SSD is a fork of Dynamic Set Dollar (DSD) and Dynamic Set Dollar is a fork of Empty Set Dollar (ESD) which is security audited by Certik. DSD and ESD currently work live without problems and security incidents. We made changes that have nothing to do with actual working of the code of ESD/DSD - just the coin name change and constant changes that you can view on Github comparison view. So we can clearly say the code is security audited, however please do your own research and check the security audit as well as the minor changes we did on Github.

Liquidity Locked

We lock 90% of SSD liquidity of our sole Uniswap liquidity pool on Team Finance. As a team we don't own any large supply of coins that may affect the price when sold. Our funding as a team comes from the remaining unlocked 10% liquidity we use overtime and carefully not to affect the price.

No Team Coins

You can check our code on Github to see that only 100 SSD is minted to deployer of the SSD contract. You can also look Etherscan history that no one in SSD team (ecosystem) owns large supply of coins that may affect the price. There is also no mint function that can be seperately used other than the one that is algorithmically used to appropriately increase the supply in case of expansion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Coupon is the method used to shrink the money supply. When the total coin supply contracts, the DAO incentivises users to voluntarily burn their SSD for coupons at a discounted SSD price depending on the debt ratio. In the event of supply extension, coupon holders will be able to redeem their coupons for SSD. Coupons expire after 45 days.

Epoch is length of time between adjustment of total SSD supply. In SSD ecosystem 1 epoch equals 2 hours, 12 times daily. Within the epoch, the coin price is measured to decide if a supply change is necessary. Holders receive rewards at the end of every epoch if it expands.

Bonding is the act of locking SSD in the Stabilized Set Dollar DAO. Holders lock their coins in the DAO in order to gain voting or reward benefits. Also, they can bond their Uniswap LP coins and earn rewards by providing liquidity. This prevents manipulations and flash loan attacks during supply expansion and contraction events.

When entering the DAO, coins must pass through a staging phase. Holders are allowed to interact with the DAO by bonding or unbonding SSD or LP just once per epoch. Staging is necessary here to control the deposit and withdrawal of coins. This is why holders has to stage SSD into the DAO, which can then be bonded.

Stabilized Set Dollar (SSD) is a slightly modified fork of Dynamic Set Dollar (DSD). DSD is modified fork of ESD and as explained in the ESD white paper, “[i]nstead of reinventing the wheel, we aim to construct [ESD] using already existing primitives where possible. This allows for minimal implementation with few unknown unknowns.” So just as ESD uses Basis and 0x Staking as it's foundation, as DSD uses ESD as it's foundation, we use DSD as our foundation because it is proven to work better than solutions.

These are the same between SSD and DSD:

- Epoch Duration of 12 epochs per day

- Rebase Amount of 3% in one epoch

- Supply / Reward Mechanism of 60% to bonded holders and 40% to liquidity providers

These are different between SSD and DSD:

- Bootstrapping period of 180 epochs aiming 1.54 USDC

- Coupon Expiry is expanded to 45 days

- DAO Exit Lockup reduced to 24 epochs

From the app, click on DAO.Deposit the coins in your balance you wish to bond into the staged area and click the plus sign, and agree to the transaction. When those coins are staged they will be available to bond. Enter coins that you wish to bond and agree to the transaction. Those coins will be bonded and they will require a 24 epoch exit lockup in order to go back to a frozen state.

Frozen and fluid tell you the state of your access when transacting with the DAO. When you are bonded and want to withdraw your bonds, you will first unbond, spend the rest of the epoch in fluid state and upon next epoch when state changes to frozen, you will be able to withdraw. For Deposit, you will be able to deposit and bond in the same epoch as when you deposit and stage you will still be in a frozen state, but as soon as you bond, you will be in a fluid state, so you cannot deposit again in that epoch, or withdraw.
